# Agent Ad Concepts

20 agent concepts, each with 6 ad variants = 120 total agent ads.

---

## Professional Agents

### 1. Clarity Check
**Color:** #059669 (Green)
**Purpose:** Catches vague language, hedging, and unclear writing.
**Insight:** "Say what you mean. Mean what you say."

| ID | Editor Text | Issue | Suggestion |
|----|-------------|-------|------------|
| clarity-vague | Our Q3 results {{demonstrate a significant improvement}} over the previous quarter. | "Significant" is vague—you have the number. Lead with it. | grew 23% |
| clarity-hedge | I {{think we might need to}} revisit the timeline before the next sprint. | Hedging weakens your point. Be direct. | We need to |
| clarity-weasel | {{Some people say}} that remote work reduces productivity. | Who says? Name them or cut it. | Critics argue |
| clarity-thing | We need to address {{the thing with}} the client's feedback. | What thing? Be specific. | the delay in responding to |
| clarity-very | The results were {{very impressive}} and exceeded our targets. | "Very" adds nothing. Show, don't tell. | exceeded targets by 40% |
| clarity-stuff | Let's discuss {{the stuff}} from yesterday's meeting. | "Stuff" is lazy. Name it. | the budget concerns |

### 2. Voice Consistency
**Color:** #7c3aed (Purple)
**Purpose:** Matches your style guide and keeps tone consistent.
**Insight:** "Your voice guide isn't decoration. It's your brand."

| ID | Editor Text | Issue | Suggestion |
|----|-------------|-------|------------|
| voice-passive | The marketing team's {{new strategy contributed to}} higher engagement. | Passive construction. Your voice guide says: active voice always. | new strategy drove |
| voice-formal | We are {{pleased to inform you}} that your request has been approved. | Too formal. Your guide says: "talk like a human." | happy to share |
| voice-jargon | Let's {{leverage our synergies}} to maximize stakeholder value. | Corporate speak alert. Your guide bans buzzwords. | work together |
| voice-hedge | I {{just wanted to check in}} about the project status. | "Just" undermines you. Your guide says: be direct. | wanted to check in |
| voice-we | {{The company has decided}} to implement new policies. | Third person feels distant. Your guide prefers "we." | We've decided |
| voice-exclaim | This is {{great news!!!!}} for the entire team! | Too many exclamation points. Your guide says: one max. | great news! |

### 3. Jargon Buster
**Color:** #ea580c (Orange)
**Purpose:** Kills corporate speak and buzzwords.
**Insight:** "If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it."

| ID | Editor Text | Issue | Suggestion |
|----|-------------|-------|------------|
| jargon-synergy | We need to {{leverage synergies}} across departments. | "Synergies" died in 2005. Let it rest. | work together |
| jargon-bandwidth | I don't have the {{bandwidth}} to take on this project. | You're not a router. Say what you mean. | time |
| jargon-circle | Let's {{circle back}} on this after the meeting. | Corporate bingo! Try human words. | revisit this |
| jargon-needle | We need to {{move the needle}} on customer satisfaction. | What needle? Speak plainly. | improve |
| jargon-low | This is {{low-hanging fruit}} for the team. | Fruit metaphors are rotten. Be direct. | easy to implement |
| jargon-eod | Please send this {{EOD}} today. | Not everyone speaks acronym. | by end of day |

### 4. Brevity
**Color:** #0284c7 (Blue)
**Purpose:** Cuts wordiness and redundancy.
**Insight:** "Brevity is the soul of wit. And emails."

| ID | Editor Text | Issue | Suggestion |
|----|-------------|-------|------------|
| brevity-order | {{In order to}} complete the project, we need more resources. | "In order to" = "To" | To |
| brevity-fact | {{Due to the fact that}} sales dropped, we're restructuring. | 5 words → 1 word. | Because |
| brevity-time | {{At this point in time}}, we're not accepting new clients. | 5 words → 1 word. | Currently |
| brevity-basic | The {{basic fundamentals}} of the strategy are sound. | Fundamentals are basic by definition. | fundamentals |
| brevity-whether | {{Whether or not}} we proceed depends on funding. | "Or not" is redundant. | Whether |
| brevity-very | This is a {{very unique}} opportunity for growth. | Unique is absolute. No "very" needed. | unique |

### 5. Confidence Booster

| ID | Editor Text | Issue | Suggestion |

### 6. Inclusive Language
**Color:** #be185d (Pink)
**Purpose:** Catches outdated or exclusionary terms.
**Insight:** "Words matter. Choose them thoughtfully."

| ID | Editor Text | Issue | Suggestion |
|----|-------------|-------|------------|
| inclusive-guys | Hey {{guys}}, here's the update on the project. | Not everyone's a guy. Try neutral. | team |
| inclusive-man | We need to {{man}} the booth at the conference. | Not a verb anyway. | staff |
| inclusive-his | Each employee should submit {{his}} timesheet by Friday. | Singular "their" works fine. | their |
| inclusive-crazy | That's a {{crazy}} idea—I love it! | "Wild" or "bold" says the same thing. | wild |
| inclusive-blind | We were {{blind}} to the risks involved. | Try "unaware"—same meaning, less baggage. | unaware of |
| inclusive-master | Push your changes to the {{master}} branch. | "Main" is the modern default. | main |

### 7. Fact Check
**Color:** #dc2626 (Red)
**Purpose:** Flags claims that need sources or verification.
**Insight:** "Trust, but verify. Then cite."

| ID | Editor Text | Issue | Suggestion |
|----|-------------|-------|------------|
| fact-studies | {{Studies show}} that productivity increases with flexibility. | Which studies? Name them or cut it. | A Stanford study found |
| fact-everyone | {{Everyone knows}} that email is dying. | Everyone? That's a big claim. | Many believe |
| fact-percent | Sales increased by {{a lot}} this quarter. | How much exactly? Numbers persuade. | 34% |
| fact-experts | {{Experts agree}} that AI will transform work. | Which experts? Be specific. | McKinsey research suggests |
| fact-always | We've {{always}} done it this way. | Since when exactly? | Since 2018, we've |
| fact-most | {{Most people}} prefer remote work. | Most? Got data? | 67% of employees |

| deadline-end | I'll send it by {{end of week}}. | Which day? Friday? Sunday? | Friday at 5pm |

### 9. Tone Matcher

| tone-eager | I would {{absolutely love}} to join the meeting! | Dial back the enthusiasm for a status call. | be happy to |
| tone-sorry | {{I'm so sorry}} for the delayed response. | One "sorry" is enough. Don't grovel. | Apologies for |

### 10. Redundancy Radar
**Color:** #0891b2 (Cyan)
**Purpose:** Catches phrases that repeat themselves.
**Insight:** "Don't say the same thing twice. Or again."

| ID | Editor Text | Issue | Suggestion |
|----|-------------|-------|------------|
| redundant-future | We need to {{plan ahead}} for the next quarter. | All planning is ahead. Drop "ahead." | plan |
| redundant-past | Let's {{revert back}} to the original design. | Revert already means back. | revert |
| redundant-free | Get a {{free gift}} with your purchase. | Gifts are free by definition. | gift |
| redundant-new | We're launching a {{new innovation}} next month. | Innovations are new by definition. | innovation |
| redundant-end | The {{end result}} exceeded expectations. | Results come at the end. | result |
| redundant-each | {{Each and every}} team member contributed. | Pick one. They mean the same thing. | Every |
